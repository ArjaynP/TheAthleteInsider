Paris 2024 Summer Olympics: What We Know?

A year from now, the 2024 Summer Olympics will be hosted in Paris, France, from July 26th, 2024, to August 11th, 2024. This makes them the second city to host the international multi-sport event three times, with their two previous events held back in 1900 and 1924. There will be precisely 329 events and 32 different sports during this timeframe. In addition, it has been confirmed that Paris will introduce 4 new sporting events, which include Olympic Skateboarding, Sport Climbing, Surfing, Olympic Breakdancing, and the Marathon race walk mixed relay.

Approximately 10,500+ athletes, along with 206 countries formally recognized as National Olympic Committees (NOCs), will be present next year. Some notable NOCs attending and favourites are the United States, China, Great Britain, Germany, and others. In the previous summer games, Tokyo 2020 was considered as a lacklustre event since it first postponed to 2021, and the entire event had no spectators due the COVID-19 pandemic. Therefore, with zero restrictions for attendance, many fans are looking forward to purchasing their tickets for the Paris summer games.

By the end of March, more than 3 million tickets were sold during the first phase of ticket sales. The draw for the second phase of tickets have already begun and will close on April 20th at 6pm Central European Time. Those that are approved will receive an email on May 9th and they must purchase their tickets within 48 hours. To learn more about purchasing tickets and scheduling events to attend, please review the official Paris Ticket Guide and the Paris 2024 calendar to learn more.
